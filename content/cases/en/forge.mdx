## How it started

I built the first version for myself. No sign-up, no database — just a tool that fit exactly how I work.

It had a journal, an AI coach that read my entries, habit tracking with streaks and XP, and a schedule I could update by just writing a message. I used it every day. It worked. Friends saw it and wanted the same thing.

## What it actually did for me

**The psychologist bot changed how I processed problems.**
Before, I'd write things down in a notebook — thoughts, blockers, things I was overthinking. It helped, but I was writing into a void. With Forge, the AI read what I wrote and responded with questions or observations based on patterns it noticed across previous entries. That feedback loop cut the time I spent stuck on a single problem from 30–40 minutes to under 10.

**Planning went from friction to automatic.**
Every week I'd spend 40–50 minutes figuring out what to do, in what order, and what was actually important versus what just felt urgent. With Forge I'd send my rough list of tasks and a few notes about the week, and the AI would return structured cards — prioritised, sorted, with time estimates. That process now takes about 8 minutes.

**Gamification made habits stick.**
Progress tracking and achievement unlocks sound gimmicky until you notice you've kept a 30-day streak without thinking about it. The XP system made consistency feel like forward motion rather than discipline.

## What changed when I opened it up

Two things had to be rebuilt:

**Personal data → user accounts.** Everything that was hardcoded for me — my tracks, my context, my journal — had to become dynamic. Each user gets their own space, their own AI memory, their own data. Supabase Auth + row-level security made sure no one sees anyone else's journal.

**Browser storage → real database.** The original version lived in localStorage. Fine for one person. Not fine when someone clears their browser and loses three months of journal entries. Supabase solved this.

Email notifications were added too — weekly summaries, streak reminders, achievement alerts.

## Launch

Two beta rounds. Real bugs found, real bugs fixed — auth issues on the first run, edge cases in the achievement system on the second. After that: stable enough to open up. Live domain, real users.

## What Forge does

**AI journal** — write about your day, the AI responds as a coach. It reads previous entries and builds a picture of patterns over time. Not generic responses — shaped by what you've actually written over weeks.

**Smart scheduling** — describe your tasks and priorities in plain language, get back a structured plan with time estimates and what actually matters flagged.

**Habit tracking + gamification** — streaks, XP, achievement badges. The kind of system that makes you want to come back tomorrow just to keep the streak alive.

**Psychology mode** — the AI uses journal context to ask the right questions at the right time. It notices themes across entries that you might not see yourself.
